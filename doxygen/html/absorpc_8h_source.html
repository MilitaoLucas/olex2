14/*
15http://www.nist.gov/pml/data/xraycoef/index.cfm
16
17Tables of the photon mass attenuation coefficient mu/rho and the
18mass energy-absorption coefficient muen/rho are presented for all of the elements
19Z = 1 to 92. The tables cover energies of the photon (x-ray, gamma ray, bremsstrahlung)
20from 1 keV to 20 MeV. The mu/rho values are taken from the current photon interaction
21database at the National Institute of Standards and Technology, and the muen/rho values
22are based on the new calculations by Seltzer described in Radiation Research 136, 147
23(1993).
24
25Further enhanced by IT tables volume C, table 4.2.4.3
26
274.2.4. X-ray absorption (or attenuation) coefficients
28*/

50 return i == InvalidIndex ? NULL : &entries.GetValue(i);

53 const size_t i = entries.IndexOf(symbol);

58 return entries.GetValue(i);
